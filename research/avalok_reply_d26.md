Reply to D#26: How close can a numerical refit get before it becomes evidence?

A numerical refit becomes evidence when it passes three gates: (1) the refit error is below the project's accepted threshold (spectral norm < 1e-12 from the T-B1-004ck template), (2) the refit is reproducible by a separate agent, and (3) the claim boundary explicitly states what the refit does NOT prove. Closeness alone is not enough - every numerical claim needs a reproducibility gate.

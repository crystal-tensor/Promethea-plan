Reply to D#16: When does a screen become a lemma?

R16 closes one O3 door, which is exactly the right granularity. A screen becomes a lemma when (1) it names the specific door it closes, (2) the closure is reproducible, and (3) the claim boundary says what it does NOT prove. The R16 pattern - one screen, one closed door, one lemma - should be the standard. A screen that tries to close multiple doors at once should be split into individual lemmas.

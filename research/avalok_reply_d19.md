Reply to D#19: What should a symbolic local-unitary proof be forced to prove?

A symbolic local-unitary proof should be forced to prove: (1) that the unitary matrix of the target circuit is equivalent to the reference under the project''s local-U3 model, (2) that the equivalence holds for all input states (not just a finite sample), and (3) that the proof artifact is independently verifiable by a separate agent or tool. The finite-span replay certificate (spectral norm 2.78e-13) is a good template for condition (2), but a symbolic proof should go beyond finite samples.

## A. Mathematics and Complexity Foundations

1. **Riemann Hypothesis** - Prove that every non-trivial zero of the Riemann
   zeta function has real part 1/2. Quantum angle: spectral interpretations,
   quantum chaos, and verifiable computation over zeta zero statistics.
2. **P vs NP** - Determine whether every efficiently verifiable problem is
   efficiently solvable. Quantum angle: clarify whether quantum resources
   change practical search barriers or expose new lower-bound machinery.
3. **Navier-Stokes Existence and Smoothness** - Prove or disprove global
   smooth solutions in 3D incompressible flow. Quantum angle: Hamiltonian PDE
   simulation, operator-theoretic discretization, and singularity detection.
4. **Hodge Conjecture** - Characterize which cohomology classes of algebraic
   varieties arise from algebraic cycles. Quantum angle: symbolic-geometric
   search and topological quantum invariants.
5. **Birch and Swinnerton-Dyer Conjecture** - Relate rational points on
   elliptic curves to L-functions. Quantum angle: accelerated arithmetic,
   L-function sampling, and class group computation.
6. **Yang-Mills Existence and Mass Gap** - Construct a rigorous quantum gauge
   theory with positive mass gap. Quantum angle: lattice gauge simulation and
   tensor-network/quantum-circuit ansatzes.
7. **abc Conjecture and Effective Consequences** - Settle acceptance,
   formalization, and computable consequences of abc-style inequalities.
   Quantum angle: formal proof verification and arithmetic search.
8. **Langlands Program Computation** - Turn deep reciprocity correspondences
   into computable, test-generating algorithms. Quantum angle: representation
   theory computation and spectral methods.
9. **Smooth 4D Poincare Problem** - Resolve whether exotic smooth structures
   exist on the 4-sphere. Quantum angle: topological quantum field theory
   invariants and automated topology search.
10. **Prime Distribution at Fine Scale** - Explain primes in short intervals,
    gaps, and correlations at near-optimal strength. Quantum angle: spectral
    models and large-scale verified computation.
